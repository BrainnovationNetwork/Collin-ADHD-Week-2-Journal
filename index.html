Week 2 Journal Prompts

These prompts are designed to help you connect the science of heart-brain coherence to your real life. There are no right or wrong answers — just honest exploration.

Your Relationship with Stress

Prompt 1: Describe your typical experience of stress. Where do you feel it in your body first? What usually triggers it? And how long does it tend to linger?
Prompt 2: Before learning about heart-brain coherence, how did you usually handle stress? What worked? What didn't? Looking back now, can you see which nervous system branch was involved?

The Heart's Intelligence

Prompt 3: Have you ever had a "gut feeling" or a "heart knowing" about something? Describe a time when your heart seemed to know something before your mind did.
Prompt 4: The heart generates an electromagnetic field that can affect the people around us. Think about someone in your life who makes you feel immediately calm and safe just by being near them. What do you think is happening energetically?

Emotions as Medicine

Prompt 5: Gratitude is one of the most powerful emotions for achieving coherence. What are 3–5 things in your life right now that you feel genuinely grateful for? As you write them, notice what happens in your body.
Prompt 6: Which positive emotion feels most accessible to you — love, gratitude, joy, appreciation, or compassion? Which feels hardest to access? What do you think gets in the way?

Brain Health & Your Future

Prompt 7: When you think about your brain health 20–30 years from now, what do you most want to preserve? What does a sharp, healthy mind mean to you?
Prompt 8: Coherence supports better sleep, and sleep is when the brain literally cleans itself. Reflect on your current relationship with sleep. What one change could you make this week?

Your Practice Journey

Prompt 9: After trying the 3-step coherence practice, describe your experience. What surprised you? What felt natural? What felt challenging?
Prompt 10: Your Coherence Commitment. Write a short personal commitment to yourself. When will you practice? What do you hope to gain?
